EuraTechnologies

More than a century of history: "from wire to fiber"

At the heart of one of the largest urban rehabilitation operations, the old industry is now home to the new digital economy.

Located in the former spinning mill (Delesalle-Desmetd factory, closed in 1989,) EuraTechnologies is a pole of excellence and innovation of digital unique in France.
After having been one of the most important sector of the country, the plant is at the heart of the conversion project of the metropolis in 1996.

In just eight years of existence, EuraTechnologies has managed to establish itself as the spearhead of the French digital economy through the French Tech.

Designed as the tool of the regional digital pole, it offers, among other things, modular spaces for rent, a digital studio, videoconferencing rooms and other technical venues.
It also has a GIX ( Global Internet eXchange ), the only in North of Paris, and access to a generalized wifi. Finally, the atrium that connects the two old buildings is equipped with plasma screens, interactive kiosks and video projectors.

EuraTechnologies supports the development of all digital entrepreneur through a methodology and unique expertise in support of companies, from their initiation to their international deployment.

A Fundacity’s study reveals that EuraTechnologies is on the podium of the most important accelerators in Europe (third). Certainly due to its strategic situation in the heart of Europe, at less than 2 hours from 8 major European cities.

International reference in terms of incubation of acceleration of startups and innovative companies, EuraTechnologies revitalizes the former mining basin and allows Lille to train in its dynamic neighboring Tourcoing, Roubaix and Loos.
